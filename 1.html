Why Music is Therapy

Music is therapy because it helps people relax, reduce stress, and improve their mood. Listening to music can release feel-good chemicals in the brain, like dopamine, which makes us feel happy. It can also lower heart rates and calm anxiety. For people with depression or trauma, music can provide comfort and a way to express emotions. Additionally, playing instruments or singing can improve focus and boost self-esteem. Music therapy is used in hospitals, schools, and clinics to help people cope with physical pain, mental health issues, and emotional challenges, making it a powerful tool for healing and well-being.
